For this thesis let us stick to Latin scripts, or think in terms of single character sets as in Latin/, Cyrillic, Greek or Devanaghari separated for this project, just for the sake of FORM.

Initially, the project of my thesis started as an intention to find the optimal EULA (End User License Agreement). The question was to find and possibly initiate a system where all foundries have a >proportionate to income revenue. Kind of like a utopian or socialist type selling system. But for that a quality control system would have to be put in place or designers would just have to accept the pricing system being put at their income ratio. You can have a little read about my intentions here or in the Initial Ideasection.

After a bit of a discussion with a few people, the answer to such a question is vast but at the same time quite easily replied to. But what came up was something in that order, but more on the front end, or design and creating part. It is also a great opportunity for me to dig deeper into this passion and look for different ways to create and stimulate my creativity. Also having in mind the current saturated font market that seems so contain all possible forms.

So is there a point going further?

The most pragmatic systems have been created, in design for designer, should_will we stopped at that? They seem to be so rational, not in a dogmatic sense but so consistently logical that it's just clear and--or_convincing.

Why go further when we have the perfect formulas?

Are they the perfect formulas or has our eye been trained that way? These systems were created for reasons that might become evident through this read beyond the obvious statement:

Hinting is automated, mastering can be, metrics all automated, spacing scripted and either you kern the font by eye and correct it through time or send it over to iKernType metrics & engineering by Igino Mariani (An engineer and designer engineer who created an algorithm that calculates the optimal spacing/kerning for your font).
It's interesting to read his manifesto

Manifesto

SPACING AND KERNING

"a typeface is a demanding task: finding a balance between designed shapes, the letters, using the white space between them requires eye and concentration, experience and effort. It’s often a tedious and overwhelming job: it can sometimes take up to 60% of the font development time.

Spacing and kerning may be a frustrating job because it’s evident there’s something repetitive in the process but it’s hard to deal with it. Time and energy remain committed resources when new designs are waiting and new projects have to be conceived."

Igino says:

60%

which is quite true and mostly enormous; given he has taken the time to program the algorithm and also analyse instead of the the type designer doing it. The plan is already mapped out here, but my main intentions of this research will start from a brief analysis of systems that I find intriguing or convincing through history. Which will bring me to analyse, try and understand system an "as close to possibly" objective analysis with a little critical output and then maybe try to present my own system, thanks to the things I have learned through the process. Maybe I will objectively try to to advocate the use of scripting — As a beginner I feel like its a faster et much more pragmatic and mathematical way of working.
